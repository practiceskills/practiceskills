Teaching Philosophy of Howard Polenberg

As a teaching professional, my philosophy is simple yet fruitful; teaching must inspire ongoing learning while also providing fun and memorable experiences. Students must be motivated and inspired by their learning experience in order to arouse an "I can't wait to get to school today and learn more" attitude from them. Vocational School teaching has given me the unique opportunity to work with students who want to learn, specifically about photography. At the Ocean County Vocational Technical School in New Jersey I am the Photography Major Instructor for the district. My students are seeking direction and may be confused by the signals they get from their comprehensive high school and adult/authority figures. I talk with my students and together we approach any situation with a "lets examine the basics and work out the components to find an answer." This approach is ideal when applied to photography and also traditional subjects such as math, which I teach as well. When teaching math I make it comprehensible - most students are afraid of math, especially without a calculator - I show them how all math can be broken down into components of simple addition, subtraction, multiplication and division and thus be applied to calculus and geometry. I find that most people learn best hands-on. This approach to the learning experience makes it interesting and fun, causing students to want to participate and practice what they have learned. It has been proven that by repetition of a task we are able to learn and retain that information. Aside from the coursework, I intend that my teaching style be a positive example that will integrate professional and practical conduct, and problem solving skills into the students repertoire of behavioral tools for ongoing success. I have consciously chosen to dedicate myself to steadily applying my philosophy of teaching to my students and my own creative endeavors.
